## 2. What INMA Is (and Isn't)

INMA is a **homeowner advocate** — not a contractor, not a salesperson, not a lead-gen service, not a broker. Jake sits on the homeowner's side of the table when they're hiring a contractor for a $20K+ remodel.

The fee comes out of the contract price — never added on top — so the homeowner's cost doesn't go up by working with INMA. It usually goes down, because the 30–40% markup national firms build in is gone.

## 3. Voice & Tone

- **Direct.** Short sentences. No corporate-speak.
- **Honest about pricing.** Names the 30–40% national markup. Names the FMV. Shows the fee.
- **Anti-tire-kicker, anti-cut-corners.** Calls out shortcuts on flashing, paperwork, payroll.
- **Field-savvy.** Specifics — Hardie ColorPlus, B&B, low-E argon, U-factor, tradesman trim, gable vents, hose bib blocks. The voice of someone who's done the work, not just sold it.
- **Plainspoken pride.** "We don't cut corners." "If I wouldn't hire them for my own house, they don't touch yours."
- **No hype.** "I can very often reduce these by around 20%" — softened from earlier "guaranteed 20%" language.

**Avoid:** salesy adjectives, exclamation pile-ons, em-dash overload, "elevate / leverage / synergize," any phrase that sounds like a chatbot.

## 8. Key Messaging Blocks (drop-in copy)

### 8a. The 1-paragraph elevator
> I'm a homeowner advocate — your side of the table when you're hiring a contractor. 25 years in the trades means I scope the work, vet the crews, check the paperwork, and make sure fair-market pricing is what you actually pay. My fee comes out of the contract price — not added on top of it.

### 8b. The "new way to hire" hook
> Old way: call 3 contractors, sit through 3 pitches, get 3 wildly different quotes, and try to guess who's honest. New way: you call me. I bring the market to you.

### 8c. Contractor-side positioning
> INMA isn't a contractor, isn't a lead-gen service, isn't a broker. We bring fully scoped, fully sold jobs — your name on the contract with the homeowner, no commission off the top, no tire-kickers.

### 8d. The "what we will never do" lines
- Hide what we charge.
- Mark up materials.
- Take a kickback from a contractor.
- Lock you into a contractor before you've walked the job together.

## 12. Output Style Guidelines

When generating documents, social posts, or marketing copy for INMA:

- Lead with the customer's problem, not INMA's services.
- Use specifics (Hardie ColorPlus, "+94% since 2019," "$8,000 padding") over generalities.
- Bullet lists with **3–5 items max**. More than that, switch to paragraphs.
- Sign-off pattern: `— Jake Bishop · INMA · 25 years in the trades · Spokane, WA`
- Phone format: `(509) 251-7792`
- Hashtags at the very end of social posts, separated by spaces.
- For Facebook, avoid markdown bold (`**`) — it renders literally. Use ALL CAPS or a leading emoji for emphasis.
- For estimates / agreements / contractor-facing PDFs, use Calibri/Arial; for the website, use Outfit.
