February 28, 2026 Rates & Materials

Lumber Prices Holding Steady — But Labor Isn't

Good news: framing lumber has stabilized around $380/MBF after the volatility we saw late last year. If you've been waiting on a siding project, material costs are about as predictable as they're going to get.

The other side of that coin — skilled labor in Spokane is getting more expensive. Two of our vetted contractors adjusted their rates in January. Nothing outrageous, but the trend is clear: waiting doesn't save you money when wages are climbing 4-6% annually.

If you're thinking about a project this spring, locking in a contractor now while you have your pick of schedules is the move. By April the good crews are booked out 6-8 weeks.

Spokane Energy Costs Are Up 4.5x — Your Siding Matters More Than You Think

Washington residential energy rates have climbed to roughly 4.5 times the national baseline. If you're in a home with original siding from the 80s or 90s, you're essentially heating the outdoors.

Modern insulated siding doesn't just look better — it can cut your heating bill by 20% or more. And here's what most people miss: Avista has rebate programs that can offset a chunk of the cost. Most contractors won't mention this because it doesn't help their bottom line.

We walk every client through available rebates and financing options before a single nail gets driven. That's the difference between a contractor who works for themselves and an agency that works for you.
