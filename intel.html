What Is the Producer Price Index?

The Producer Price Index (PPI) is published monthly by the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics. It measures what manufacturers and producers charge for goods before they reach you. For building materials, it tracks the wholesale cost of lumber, glass, metals, paint, concrete, and other construction inputs.

The composite score above compares the latest value of each material category to its 12-month average. A score of 5 means costs are at their recent norm. Above 5 means materials are running expensive. Below 5 means favorable pricing.

How to Read the Score

1-3: Favorable
