What Is the Producer Price Index?

The Producer Price Index (PPI) is published monthly by the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics. It measures what manufacturers and producers charge for goods before they reach you. For building materials, it tracks the wholesale cost of lumber, concrete, metals, glass, siding, and other construction inputs.

When the PPI is above its 12-month moving average, it means material costs are trending higher than their recent norm — projects started during this period will likely cost more. When it's below the moving average, costs are easing and you may get more favorable pricing.

This isn't a prediction. It's a snapshot of where prices actually are right now relative to where they've been.

How to Read the Trend

Three scenarios and what they suggest for project timing:

Above Average

Material costs are running higher than recent norms. Projects priced today may cost more than they would have 6 months ago. Consider locking in quotes if you're ready.

Near Average

Costs are in line with their recent trend. No urgency, but no reason to wait either. A neutral environment for planning and pricing.

Below Average

Material costs are trending lower than usual. Favorable conditions for starting a project. This doesn't last forever — good time to move.
