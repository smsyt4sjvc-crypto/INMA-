The behavioral half of the edge. [[where-the-edge-is]] / [[how-to-get-paid]] answer *where* the money is
(get PAID to bear risk — VRP, regime-gated). This note answers *how you keep it*: survival discipline.

## DATA (observed — the checkable core)
- **Black Monday, Oct 19 1987: the Dow fell −22.6% in a single day** — still the largest one-day % loss in
  US market history. (Historical fact.)
- **"Trader" (PBS, 1987)** is a real ~1hr documentary filmed months before the crash, showing Paul Tudor Jones
  (then ~32) and research partner Peter Borish overlaying a 1929-analog chart on 1987. The film exists on
  YouTube. (Real; PTJ reportedly did seek to restrict its circulation — the "40 years / legal effort" framing
  is viral-post embellishment, treat as story not fact.)
- **Tudor Investment Corp** — founded 1980; PTJ still active. The paste's "≈19%/yr for 45 years" is a
  round-number CLAIM from a social post, NOT a verified track-record pull — ⚠️ do not cite as fact.
- **The signature quote (his, repeated across his career):** *"The most important rule of trading is to play
  great defense, not great offense."*
- **The stated method in the film:** build conviction slowly → size carefully → strike hard when the setup
  arrives; **tight stops, cut positions fast, never average down on a loser**, don't trust yourself in a
  drawdown. Same template repeated, not random bets.

## THESIS (interpretation — NOT fact)
- *(analysis — this is the behavioral complement to our structural edge)* The vault's edge is STRUCTURAL: get
  paid the volatility-risk premium, gated by the 200-day ([[where-the-edge-is]]). PTJ supplies the BEHAVIORAL
  layer that keeps a structural edge from being blown up by a single tail: **defense > offense.** A positive-
  expectancy system (VRP seller ~82-84% win rate) still dies if one un-cut loser is sized to ruin. The two
  halves are one system: *edge in the setup, survival in the sizing.*
- *(the money line for THIS book)* **"Greatness is a refusal, not a talent."** Refuse to be reckless, refuse to
  be certain, refuse to average down, refuse to trust yourself in a drawdown. Every one of those refusals is
  already encoded as a LAW in the trading system: defined-risk-only, the daily-loss kill-switch + HALT, name-
  quality/no-earnings, position limits ([[../trading-system/constitution.md]] laws-as-code). PTJ is the human
  proof that the laws ARE the edge, not a constraint on it.
- *(the direct hit on Jake's own error log)* The MNST post-mortem ([[portfolio-state]]): held a losing put for
  **weeks** while MNST rode an overbought RSI against his own GLP-1 / sugar-free thesis. That is the textbook
  violation of "cut fast / never average down on a loser / don't trust yourself in a drawdown." PTJ's discipline
  is the precise antidote to Jake's one recurring, documented leak (the "fade-strength / marry-the-thesis"
  pattern). This note exists so the next time a position goes against the thesis and Jake feels the urge to
  hold "just a bit longer," the refusal is written down where he'll see it.
- *(what it does NOT license)* It does NOT endorse crash-calling via historical analogs (WARNING-vs-TRIGGER: a
  chart-overlay is a state-pattern, not a dated trigger). PTJ won by managing risk *around* an uncertain call,
  not by the call being magic. Import the defense, not the crystal ball.
