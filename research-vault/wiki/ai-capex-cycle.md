# AI Capex Cycle

The fundamental engine under [[market-fragility]]. Related: [[cepi]], [[power-not-petroleum]].

> Firewall: DATA = measured/sourced. THESIS = interpretation.

## DATA (observed)
- Leadership concentration: ~11 names ≈ 40% of S&P weight; AI-capex complex is the core.
  *(session cells)*
- Capex-vs-customer-revenue "air gap" estimated ~12x at the hyperscaler layer; trajectory
  crosses to **negative FCF-after-buybacks ~2026** on current spend plans. *(session synthesis
  — estimate, not a single audited figure; see THESIS)*
- Circular financing observed: chip/model/cloud vendors funding each other's demand
  (vendor financing, equity stakes, prepaid capacity). *(user-supplied sourced report)*
- Power/grid is the physical bottleneck for datacenter buildout → ties to [[power-not-petroleum]].

## THESIS (interpretation — NOT fact)
- *(user's thesis)* Once AI is adopted everywhere, token usage gets more efficient; if firms
  invest to replace inefficient *labor*, capital will equally invest to replace inefficient
  *compute* — so compute demand is not a one-way ramp, and the capex is being sized to a
  demand curve that efficiency will bend. The bezzle (Burry) is in the depreciation schedules
  and circular revenue.
- *(analysis)* The ~12x air-gap and 2026 FCF-crossover figures are synthesized estimates from
  spend guidance vs disclosed AI revenue — directionally supported, not a single reported
  number. Keep them in THESIS until each leg is sourced under DATA.

## Counterweights (steelman the other side)
- *(analysis)* Hyperscalers have real cash flows and can fund capex from operations far longer
  than a classic bubble; demand could be under- not over-estimated if agents/inference scale.
  This is why the [[fragility-engine]] falsifier "air gap closes with revenue" matters.
