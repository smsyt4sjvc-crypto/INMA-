# Buying at All-Time Highs — the framework

Written 2026-07-09 after Jake's question: "how do I price anything when everything is constantly at
the peak?" — with MSFT −19% YTD and NVDA flat both looking "cheap vs their own history."
Related: [[concentration]], [[quiet-health-screen]], [[ai-capex-cycle]], [[cepi]], [[portfolio-state]].

> Firewall: framework = analysis. The MSFT/NVDA reads = interpretation, labeled. Descriptive, not advisory.

## 1. The peak is an aggregation artifact — price businesses, not the index
- The INDEX at highs ≠ its components at highs. The 7/5 top-10 print: MSFT −18.9% YTD, META −11.5%,
  NVDA +4.6% (stalled) while the other 490 carried the index ([[concentration]]).
- **Rule: the index level is the least informative number on the screen.** "Trading at all-time highs"
  is never the decision; one company's earnings path at one price is the decision.
- Empirical footnote: buying the index at ATHs has historically produced ~average forward returns —
  highs beget highs more often than they mark tops. ATH is not a sell signal; it's a "this number
  can't help you" signal.

## 2. "Cheap vs its own history" is only cheap if the E survives
- A lower multiple on a fallen stock = bargain ONLY if earnings hold. The test already run three times:
  LULU (E eroding = trap), BF-B (trap), GILD (accounting optics + RAISED sales guide = real).
- **"Cheap vs the last several years" assumes the last several years' regime persists.** The GPT-reframe
  says the regime changed: the market reprices BUILDERS until capex ROI shows up (Bain: <10%). A
  compressed multiple caused by your own thesis is not a mispricing of your thesis.

## 3. The capex cliff hits "cheap" names asymmetrically (the MSFT-vs-NVDA split)
- *(interpretation)* **NVDA loses its REVENUE on the cliff** — hyperscaler capex IS its income statement.
  Its "historically low multiple" is potentially the **peak-cycle-earnings trap** (cheap multiple on
  top-of-cycle E — the CF/EOG screener lesson, applied to the GPU king). Plus Burry's one-customer
  concentration + custom-silicon share loss.
- *(interpretation)* **MSFT loses its biggest EXPENSE on the cliff** — a builder that stops building gets
  a free-cash-flow explosion. The market punished the spending (−19%); discipline gets rewarded (mirror
  of Meta punished for raising). **On the cliff: NVDA = casualty, MSFT = closer to beneficiary.**
- Forever-hold framing: MSFT = diversified cash machine where AI is one expensive bet; NVDA = single
  product line at record share/margins whose customers are building its replacement. Both buyable —
  not with the same logic, size, or patience.

## 4. Mechanics for accumulating at index highs (tools already built)
1. **The [[quiet-health-screen]]** — the 9% of the index cheap on FILED cash flow with no story attached.
   That list IS "what to buy at the peak."
2. **Rungs, not bottom calls.** The GTC-ladder structure was right; the error was rungs on names whose E
   was later doubted. Pick names where the E is trusted (that's the whole decision), set tranche prices
   defensible in a year, let red days fill them.
3. **Sizing carries the uncertainty** — under-exposure while learning isn't weakness; it's where
   uncertainty belongs (the current book: cash + hedge + 2 forever-shares).

## The compressed version
**The index peak is noise. The E-path is everything. The capex cliff feeds one "cheap" name's FCF and
eats the other's revenue — "cheap" is not one category.**
