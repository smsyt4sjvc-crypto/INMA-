# Civilian-infrastructure strike log (US-Iran war) — two ledgers, by side

A running, dated record of strikes on **non-military / civilian-impact infrastructure** — power, water,
desalination, telecom, bridges, airports, ports, refining (domestic fuel), and civilian casualties.
**Excluded:** purely military targets (bases, naval, missile/drone sites, radar, the anti-ship kill chain).
Oil/energy nodes that are *export*-facing are tracked in [[demand-destruction]] (the oil marker); they appear
here only when the impact is domestic-civilian (e.g. a refinery serving domestic fuel).

> Firewall: this is a DATA ledger — each line = a dated, as-reported strike + source + credibility tag.
> The "how to read" section at the bottom is the only THESIS.
> **Info-war discipline (per the CENTCOM lesson):** Iranian-agency wires (Tasnim/Fars/Mehr/ISNA/IRIB = IRGC-
> linked) are **CLAIMS** until neutral/official confirmation. Victim-state confirmations (e.g. Kuwait KUNA /
> MoD of its own damage) are **credible**. Tag every line: ✅ confirmed / ⚠️ claim / ◻︎ threat-not-strike.

Related: [[demand-destruction]] (oil marker + full war chronology), [[market-fragility]].

## How to read it (the only THESIS section)
- **Credibility is asymmetric, and that asymmetry is itself information.** Ledger A leans heavily on
  Iranian-agency wires (Tasnim/Mehr/ISNA/Fars), which have a motive to *allege* civilian damage (frames the US
  as hitting civilians → internationalizes the war, justifies escalation). Ledger B leans on **victim states
  confirming their own damage** (Kuwait KUNA/MoD), which is credible and hard to fake. So Ledger B's civilian
  hits are better-established than Ledger A's claimed ones — weight accordingly, don't read the two ledgers as
  equal-confidence.
- **Dual-use caveat:** power/water/telecom grids also feed military sites, so "civilian" is genuinely contested
  for some entries — a strike can be civilian-impact AND militarily-motivated. Tagged, not adjudicated.
- **The escalation ladder this tracks (per WARNING-vs-TRIGGER):** military → oil/energy → **civilian utilities**
  is a rung-climb. The appearance of power/water/desal targets on *both* ledgers (7/14 Kish claim; 7/17-18
  Kuwait confirmed) = the war crossing into civilian-infrastructure warfare. It raises humanitarian + political
  stakes; it is **not** by itself an oil-supply (crude-export) event — that distinction is kept in
  [[demand-destruction]] so this ledger doesn't smuggle a market-timing claim into a humanitarian tracker.
- Keep appending dated lines; correct visibly (strike-through, don't delete) if a claim is later debunked
  (e.g. the 7/17 CENTCOM-denied Tasnim US-KIA claim pattern).
