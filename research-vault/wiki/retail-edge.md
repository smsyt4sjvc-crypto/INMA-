> Analysis, drawn from heavily-documented results (tier-3 knowledge per [[_calibration]]) + the

## The documented winner
Uninterrupted low-cost broad-index accumulation: ~90% of professional large-cap funds lose to the
index over 15yrs (SPIVA); retail's realized returns lag even the funds held by 1.5-2%/yr from timing
behavior (Morningstar Mind-the-Gap / Dalbar). **The binding constraint on retail ROI is behavior, not
information.** The best system removes the operator from the loop → the two-bucket core.

## The four structural advantages retail edges must exploit (institutions cannot copy these)
1. **No forced selling** — can hold through redemption-driven bottoms.
2. **No career risk** — can hold what fires PMs; can look wrong for quarters.
3. **Size** — can buy $0.4-10B names (where [[runner-anatomy]] found everything).
4. **No mandate** — can hold cash and deploy only into the ~4 panic windows/decade.
Every documented retail success = one of these worn as a strategy. The book's current architecture
uses all four (core = remove self; cash+lists = mandate; cluster hunting = size; hold-quarters = horizon).

## Information actually needed
Core: ~none (by design). Active sleeves: verified primary sources (filings > headlines), positioning
extremes (stored energy), insider records (the one leading dataset), and SELF-information (the bias
map) — the last being the binding one per the behavior-gap evidence.

## Historical applicability (the decay question)
- **Published tradeable anomalies decay** (~50%+ post-publication, McLean & Pontiff); insider signal
  thinner post-OpenInsider.
- **Behavioral/structural mechanisms persist** (momentum ~200yrs post-publication; panic-buying stays
  hard forever) because institutions structurally cannot arbitrage them.
- **Mechanisms travel; parameters don't.** The LEAST applicable dataset today may be 2010-2020
  (ZIRP/QE/buy-every-dip) if the regime flipped — mechanism-level findings (wreckage+intact-business,
  fear duration, supply responses killing memory cycles) hold; parameter-level ones (exact thresholds,
  dip-buy expectancy) are regime-hostages. Frame all studies at mechanism level (runner-anatomy did).
- Caveat: robust base rates manufacture their own exceptions — 0/14 fear stat is both the best rule
  available and the complacency-trainer that eventually breaks it.

## 2026-07-14 ~6:22am PT — "buy the lowest open" = bottom-picking; the tool is a limit LADDER
- Jake: "how do I buy at the lowest possible market open?" *(execution doctrine)*
- **You can't reliably hit the open's low** — noisiest/widest-spread window; the first 1-5 min are
  the opening auction (whipsaw). A MARKET-ON-OPEN order fills at the auction price = near the HIGH
  on a gap-up day = the opposite of the goal.
- **The tools that serve "buy low" honestly:** (1) LIMIT order, never market — cap the price, enforce
  no-chase. (2) LADDER/scale-in: limit buys at descending support levels (⅓/⅓/⅓) → fill more the
  lower it goes, average beats any single guess, never chase THE low. (3) Let the opening range settle
  (first 15-30 min) before committing.
- **Gap-up inverts the premise:** MU +5% pre-market = today's open is a gap UP. "Buy the lowest open"
  on a gap-up = buy the RIP (the exact anti-pattern). The dip-entry may not be TODAY. The ladder
  handles it: no fill = a WIN for a buy-the-dip investor, not a miss. Runway is a month — catch A dip,
  not THIS open. Ties [[_calibration]] (concentration/FOMO), [[new-economy-regime]] (dated runway).
