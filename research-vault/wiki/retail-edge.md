2. **No career risk** — can hold what fires PMs; can look wrong for quarters.
3. **Size** — can buy $0.4-10B names (where [[runner-anatomy]] found everything).
4. **No mandate** — can hold cash and deploy only into the ~4 panic windows/decade.
Every documented retail success = one of these worn as a strategy. The book's current architecture
uses all four (core = remove self; cash+lists = mandate; cluster hunting = size; hold-quarters = horizon).

## Information actually needed

extremes (stored energy), insider records (the one leading dataset), and SELF-information (the bias
map) — the last being the binding one per the behavior-gap evidence.

## Historical applicability (the decay question)
- **Published tradeable anomalies decay** (~50%+ post-publication, McLean & Pontiff); insider signal
  thinner post-OpenInsider.
- **Behavioral/structural mechanisms persist** (momentum ~200yrs post-publication; panic-buying stays
  hard forever) because institutions structurally cannot arbitrage them.
- **Mechanisms travel; parameters don't.** The LEAST applicable dataset today may be 2010-2020
  (ZIRP/QE/buy-every-dip) if the regime flipped — mechanism-level findings (wreckage+intact-business,
  fear duration, supply responses killing memory cycles) hold; parameter-level ones (exact thresholds,
  dip-buy expectancy) are regime-hostages. Frame all studies at mechanism level (runner-anatomy did).
- Caveat: robust base rates manufacture their own exceptions — 0/14 fear stat is both the best rule
  available and the complacency-trainer that eventually breaks it.
