## THESIS (interpretation — Jake's, NOT fact)
COVID detached the felt value of money and labor. A whole cohort now sits *below its reference
point* (priced out of homes/retirement — the Hollow Bottom) AND believes the numéraire is
eroding (debasement / Fed Trap) AND watches AI devalue labor ("why grind"). Result: a
macro-scale shift into the **risk-seeking region of the utility curve**. People ignore inherent
EV — not by miscalculating it, but because downside stopped *feeling* like loss and the status
quo feels hopeless. They prefer variance. The melt-up is not classic exuberance; it is
**nihilistic / nothing-to-lose risk-seeking**.

## Why it's theoretically real (not just a mood)
- **Prospect theory reflection effect:** humans are risk-*seeking* in the domain of losses. A
  cohort that perceives itself as behind gambles to get back to reference (the get-out-of-the-hole trade).
- **Lottery / positive-skew demand:** when a saved dollar can't buy the life goal but a lottery
  payoff could, the utility function goes convex — people *rationally* pay for variance (OTM
  calls, low-price stocks, crypto, memecoins).
- **Eroding numéraire:** if cash feels like a melting ice cube, holding it feels like losing →
  might as well swing. This is the debasement thesis expressed at the retail behavioral level.

## Why it matters (integrates, doesn't refute)
- **Bull:** supplies the marginal, loss-aversion-free buyer that *extends* the melt-up —
  consistent with Jake's own "2021 analog, break extends to ~Feb-Mar 2027."
- **Does NOT refute the crash:** it *feeds* mechanical-leverage fragility (Vector 11). A buyer
  with no stop = bigger melt-up, then harder break. Retimes it later, deepens it worse.
- **Book implication:** argues for the exact barbell already on — QLD/high-beta long to ride the
  Bid, longer-dated puts for the deeper break.
