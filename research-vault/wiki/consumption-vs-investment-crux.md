# The Consumption-vs-Investment Crux (spine)

The one question that sits ABOVE the 11-vector convergence stack and sorts every position.
Related: [[market-fragility]], [[detachment-bid]], [[ai-capex-cycle]], [[_calibration]], [[portfolio-state]].

## The question
Did the post-COVID borrowing **BUILD** (investment → mortgage → debt inflates away against a
bigger, more productive economy) or **DRINK** (consumption → hangover → the tab comes due)?
Every bear vector bets *drink*; every bull vector ([[detachment-bid]], AI-productivity) bets *build*.

## It is NOT a binary — two axes (the key upgrade, Jake 2026-07-01)
1. **Build vs drink** — aggregate. Did output/productivity actually rise?
2. **Build for whom** — distribution. Who captured the gain?
These decouple. Aggregate productivity CAN genuinely rise (bull macro: debt/GDP falls, inflates
away) WHILE the median household is destroyed (bear distribution: Hollow Bottom deepens) —
*no contradiction*, because when labor has no leverage the gain accrues entirely to capital.
**AI doesn't have to fail for the Hollow Bottom to be real; it has to succeed — for capital.**

## DATA — why the 2020-21 tranche was consumption (near-identity, not counterfactual)
- Production was physically **stopped** (factories offline — SF Bay, Mexico, global supply chain).
  MV=PQ with Q constrained → the money landed in **prices, not output**. Stimulus into a
  supply-halted economy is consumption *by definition*: nothing to invest *in*.
- Fingerprint confirms: wealth concentration UP, real wages DOWN, inflation outran wages. An
  investment boom flows productivity to wages; this did the opposite.

## The engine — the reflexive labor loop
Borrowed money repriced labor **UP** (PPP, enhanced UI, wage bidding war vs free money) at the
exact moment zero rates repriced its **replacement DOWN** (automation/AI ROI: "someday" → "this
quarter"). Labor took out a loan to buy the machine that fires it. Timing for labor was "about
as bad as you could manufacture with intent" — repriced up + stripped of leverage (remote work
exposed which roles were theater) exactly as the cheaper input arrived.

## The iron law
**Math is math. Capital doesn't compromise.** Once the cost curve crosses, substitution is a
spreadsheet, not a negotiation — no malice, no mercy. This is why [[detachment-bid]] and the
Hollow Bottom coexist: capital routes to AI (math), labor loses leverage (math), the displaced
turn to nihilistic risk-seeking (what else) — one internally consistent, grim system.

## The open question that decides the book (the Steelman lives here)
Every prior automation wave (farm→factory→office) looked this catastrophic and **reabsorbed**
labor onto a higher rung nobody could see in advance. The Luddites were right the looms took the
jobs, wrong that there was no next rung. So: **this time, is there a higher rung — or did AI take
the ladder?** Bear answer already in the stack (Vector 3 / SaaSpocalypse: the substitute is
*cognition itself*, no higher cognitive ground to retreat to). Unfalsifiable until it isn't →
which is *why you hold the barbell* instead of betting the farm either way.

## The proposed bridge (Jake's prescription)
[[ioss-proposal]] is Jake's attempt to *build the valve* on the distribution axis: route part of
the capital-side productivity gain back to labor (gains-only, above-baseline, 70/30) without
killing the investment incentive. Its worker-equity option is the part that could actually
re-couple labor to productivity — i.e., help labor reach the "higher rung" by becoming capital.

## Political corollary (feeds the Fed Trap)
The politician who **defers** the recession gets the credit; the one holding when the tab arrives
gets the blame. So the structural incentive is *always* to defer — nobody pays for the recession
they caused, only the one they failed to kick down the road. → "he'll inflate anyhow" base case.

## How to use it (per position)
For every holding ask: **am I betting hangover or mortgage — and for whom?** Bull-aggregate +
bear-distribution can both be right, so a position can be correct on productivity and still ride
a society that's hollowing. Size accordingly.
