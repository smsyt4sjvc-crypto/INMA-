## THESIS (interpretation — NOT fact)
- *(analysis)* SMRs are the *proposed fix* for the datacenter power bottleneck that's stalling the
  buildout ([[ai-capex-cycle]]: 16GW→5GW; grid/transformer limits). "Ideal for data centers" is the
  whole pitch — dedicated behind-the-meter firm power. US 28-announcement lead = real industrial/policy
  tailwind for the theme.
- *(⚠️ calibration — the key check)* **"Siting announcements" = intent, not steel in the ground.**
  SMRs are largely **pre-commercial**; first US units realistically ~2028-2030+, and the sector has a
  cancellation history (NuScale/UAMPS 2023). So this is a **narrative / leading** signal, NOT near-term
  earnings. OKLO/SMR/NNE are **optionality** (Tier C sizing), not cash flows.
- *(timing split — useful)* Near-term AI power scarcity favors **existing** generation (CEG/VST/GEV/gas)
  — SMRs are too slow to fix the 2026-27 bottleneck. SMRs are the **2028+** leg. Don't conflate the two:
  own existing nukes/IPPs for the near-term squeeze, hold SMR developers as long-dated optionality.

## Falsifier
- SMR timelines slip further / another marquee cancellation → developer names (OKLO/SMR/NNE) de-rate;
  thesis shifts entirely to existing generation. Watch first-concrete / NRC-approval milestones, not announcements.
