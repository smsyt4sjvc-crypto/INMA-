# Oil value chain — where the money actually goes

Answers Jake's Q (2026-07-20): "With all the oil the US is pumping, where's the money going — Exxon? Chevron?
Refineries?" The structural map of who captures the economics, by layer.

Related: [[power-not-petroleum]], [[demand-destruction]] (the crack thread + the suppression stack),
[[where-the-edge-is]] ("own the toll" meta-pattern).

> Firewall: DATA = the layers + mechanics + who's in them. THESIS = where value accrues + the read.

## DATA (observed — structural)
- **US = world's largest producer:** ~13M+ bpd crude, ~20M+ bpd total liquids (with natural-gas liquids, NGLs).
  Record output. US exports ~4M bpd crude + more refined product.
- **Oil is priced GLOBALLY** (Brent-linked). A US producer takes the world price for every barrel; pumping MORE
  can push that price DOWN. US consumers pay the world price + the refining/logistics margin — no domestic discount.
- **The four layers:**
  1. **Upstream / producers** — integrated majors **XOM, CVX**; shale independents **EOG, FANG (Diamondback),
     OXY, COP, DVN, CTRA**. Take the flat-crude price.
  2. **Downstream / refiners** — **VLO (Valero), MPC (Marathon), PSX (Phillips 66)** + the majors' refining arms.
     Capture the **crack spread** = product price out − crude cost in. (3-2-1 crack ~$69 vs ~$23 2018-25 avg during
     the July-2026 war — see [[demand-destruction]] oil scan.)
  3. **Midstream / transport** — **EPD, ET, WMB (Williams), KMI, OKE, PAA**. **Fee/volume-based tolls**, largely
     price-insensitive. (Blackstone $5.34B into Williams power JVs — buying the toll booth.)
  4. **Oilfield services** — **HAL, SLB, BKR**. Paid on drilling ACTIVITY (rig count / frac spreads).
- **Capital-discipline shift (post-2020):** 2010s shale = negative free cash flow, debt-funded growth (cash
  incinerated → enriched Wall Street + services, not investors). 2020s = FCF generated and RETURNED (buybacks +
  dividends) + **consolidation** (XOM bought Pioneer ~$60B; CVX bought Hess).

## THESIS (interpretation — NOT fact)
- *(where the money goes — NOT a producer gusher)* Record production ≠ record producer profits. It flows to:
  (1) **shareholders** — disciplined producers return cash via buybacks/dividends (and asset owners skew wealthy →
  the same debasement/inequality dynamic, [[new-economy-regime]]); (2) **refiners** — the crack, ESPECIALLY now
  (war + tight products = record 3-2-1 while flat crude fades → refiners minted the war money, producers didn't);
  (3) **midstream** — steady volume tolls regardless of price. NOT into a reinvestment gusher; NOT into cheaper US
  gas (you pay the global price + the crack — why $4 gas coexists with record US output).
- *(the "own the toll, not the barrel" read — the session's 5th landing on this)* The **barrel price is a coin
  flip** (global, war-whipped, demand-destructing). The durable capture is the **crack** (refiners) and the
  **toll** (midstream) — the layers paid regardless of who wins the price war. Same meta-pattern as Nvidia (runs
  the tokens American or Chinese), Watsco (HVAC distribution hot summer or not), the CTA mechanical layer. Money is
  never in the thing everyone watches; it's in the layer everyone must pay to move it.
- *(tie-back — US production caps its own price)* US record supply IS part of the "suppression stack"
  ([[demand-destruction]]) that absorbs the war premium every time crude probes $90 — the thing being pumped is the
  thing capping the pumper's price. Producers are structurally short their own volume's price impact.
- *(descriptive — NOT a buy rec)* Maps where value accrues by layer; sizing/execution is Jake's. The applicable
  read: if an energy name tempts, ask which LAYER it is — crack/toll (capture) vs barrel (coin flip + global price
  + return-not-reinvest).
