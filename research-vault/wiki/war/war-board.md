## THE FLAG (one per ledger entry — grep these tokens to scan)
- 🔴 **`[MARKER-MOVED]`** — actually moved oil or the market (Brent gap, executed strike on export infra, executed
  chokepoint closure). The real signal. **A scan for this = "what actually changed the price."**
- 🟠 **`[SHOULD-MOVE]`** — *analytically should* move the market but it HASN'T yet. **The divergence / mispricing flag —
  the alpha bucket.** A scan for this = "where the market may be underpricing the war."
- 🟡 **`[ODDS-SHADER]`** — shifts a trigger's PROBABILITY; times nothing (a WARNING-state, per the WARNING-vs-TRIGGER rule).
- ⚪ **`[TEXTURE]`** — logged for completeness; no market relevance (most rhetoric).

**Scan command (find everything that matters):** grep `MARKER-MOVED\|SHOULD-MOVE` across `wiki/war/`.

## THE TRIGGERS (what ACTUALLY prices oil — the fuse, unchanged since day 1)
The war-marker razor: **words/threats don't hold crude up; only a physical export cutoff does.** Four uncapping tells:
- **(a) Export-terminal strike** — Saudi/Gulf crude EXPORT/processing node hit (Abqaiq-class, or Kharg). → the catastrophic
  uncap, Brent $120-147. **THE trigger.**
- **(b) Hull-stopped / abandoned vessel** — a tanker physically stopped/abandoned in the strait (not just rerouted).
- **(c) Chokepoint closure — EXECUTED** — Hormuz OR Bab-el-Mandeb actually shut (kinetic block, mining, or the
  NON-kinetic route: broad insurer adoption of the Lloyd's toll-voids-cover clause).
- **(d) Brent physical gap through $90 → $100 → sustained** — the price itself confirming a real cutoff, not premium.
- **OFF-RAMP:** a credible ceasefire / de-escalation announcement, or a fresh accepted proposal → premium bleeds, oil down.
