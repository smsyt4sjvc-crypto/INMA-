## Definition (fact)
- **Demand destruction** = consumption that goes away *permanently* (behavior/tech/structural
  change), and does NOT return when price falls — distinct from ordinary price elasticity,
  which reverses. Curve shifts left, not just slides down.

## DATA (the bull counter — 2026-07-01, ZeroHedge)
- "Next Oil Rally Could Be Driven By Stockpile Refilling": IEA to refill ~400M bbl released in
  the crisis; India + major importers expanding reserves → latent new demand once Mideast subsides.
- As-reported context: total US crude incl. SPR at lowest since June 2025; Trump (G7) said
  reserves run out "in ~4 weeks"; **Cushing ~20M bbl 'tank bottoms', lowest since Oct 2014.**
  *(`raw/zerohedge-2026-07-01-grid-emergency-oil-refill.md`)*
- ⚠️ **Reconciliation:** this depleted-inventory / refill narrative is in tension with HFI's
  *current-glut / contango* read above. Likely different clocks: HFI = near-term physical glut
  (China demand + Hormuz unwind) now; refill story = latent demand once reserves must be rebuilt.
  Both can be true in sequence. Do not treat either as the settled state of oil.

## THESIS (interpretation — NOT fact)
- *(analysis)* The China import collapse is the structural (durable) leg; the supply increase +
  Hormuz unwind is the transient leg. Oil is weak *despite* an active Middle East conflict
  because the physical market is glutted and Chinese demand is structurally lower (EVs,
  property bust, and diesel→LNG truck switch).
- *(analysis)* The diesel→LNG truck switch is demand destruction for the *barrel* that is
  demand *creation* for the *molecule* → hardens [[power-not-petroleum]].
- *(analysis, caution)* HFI is likely structurally *bullish* crude on a multi-year horizon
  (supply underinvestment) even while acknowledging this near-term glut. Near-term weakness
  ≠ oil can never spike again; a supply shock can still jump a soft-demand market.
- *(analysis, 2026-07-01)* Stockpile-refilling is the concrete mechanism for that latent bull
  case: emptied SPR/Cushing + IEA/India rebuild = a demand source independent of consumption.
  Tension with "power, not petroleum" ([[power-not-petroleum]]): the toll-not-drill-bit read is
  about *equities/flows now*; a refill-driven crude rally could still lift the whole complex
  (incl. the producers currently being dumped). Watch: if Cushing draws + crude backwardates,
  the oil-bearish leg weakens — that's the falsifier for the demand-destruction lean.
