# SemiAnalysis — "Stop Saying Half of 2026 US Datacenter Capacity Is Canceled" (2026-06-18)

Pasted by Jake 2026-07-12 (full article). SemiAnalysis (Dylan Patel's shop) — datacenter-industry
research firm with a satellite-imagery "Vision Model" tracking physical construction. This is the
direct rebuttal to the viral "~50% of 2026 DC capacity canceled/delayed" claim the vault ingested
2026-07-01 (`raw/zerohedge-2026-07-01-datacenter-cancellations.md`, sourced to Bloomberg/Sightline
Climate).

## The core claim
- The viral 50%-canceled number traces to Bloomberg citing **Sightline Climate: ~12GW expected 2026
  starts, only ~5GW under construction.** SemiAnalysis: both numbers are **"off by multiples"** vs
  their satellite-verified tracking — the denominator (expected starts) is inflated by a speculative
  "phantom pipeline" and the numerator (under construction) badly undercounts real activity.
- **SemiAnalysis's own YE2026 hyperscaler self-build capacity forecast moved ~1% over the last 6
  months.** Colocation forecast moved <5%. I.e., the real 2026 pipeline is essentially intact.

## The phantom pipeline (the denominator problem)
- Interconnection queues are full of speculative land plays that were never going to be built:
  **ERCOT queue ~410GW of large-load requests vs ~85GW system peak demand → ~311GW+ "phantom"** by
  any reasonable read. Developers file multiple sites to option power, build one (or none).
- Cancellations/delays cluster overwhelmingly in this early-stage speculative tier — projects with
  no signed tenant, no equipment orders, often no land closing. Killing them removes nothing from
  real 2026 capacity.

## Real delays documented (the article's honest side)
- **STACK/Oracle "Stargate" New Mexico: pushed to ~2029** — NMED air permits (turbine emissions) +
  FERC Section 7 review on the supply pipeline. Vs Oracle's guided 1H2027 capacity. (= a REAL,
  large, named delay on the exact ORCL delivery path.)
- **Nebius New Jersey: slipped ~5 months.**
- **Core Scientific Denton TX: missed YE2025 target; CoreWeave took over the project; H1'26 now
  on track.**
- Moratoriums/NIMBY actions overwhelmingly land in areas with no real projects — with the notable
  real-project exceptions the vault already logged (QTS Virginia; and now STACK/Oracle NM).

## Equipment-supplier narrative (the part that matters for the bottleneck map)
- Canceled speculative projects **never placed equipment orders** — so cancellations don't free up
  transformer/switchgear slots the way the bear narrative implies.
- **Reinhausen (tap-changer bushings) quoting 3–5 YEARS.** Primary hyperscaler electrical suppliers
  **sold out through 2027.** Vertiv/Schneider margins >20% and holding.
- 3–4 year order queues mean any genuinely canceled slot is instantly reallocated to the next buyer
  in line — backlogs are locked regardless of individual project mortality.

## Industry adaptations (how the buildout routes around constraints)
- Behind-the-meter (BtM) generation; "powered land" acquisitions; prefab/modular construction.
- **Chinese OEMs quietly relieving high-end electrical equipment bottlenecks: xAI likely using
  Sieyuan transformers/breakers; flow via US brokers AND direct hyperscaler purchases; accelerating
  over the past 12 months.**
