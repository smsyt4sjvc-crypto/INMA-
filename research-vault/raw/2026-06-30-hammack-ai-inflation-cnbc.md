# CNBC (Jeff Cox) — Hammack: "insatiable" AI demand could fuel inflation; hikes may be necessary

Pasted by Jake 2026-07-10. CNBC interview (Sara Eisen), sidelines of ECB conference, Sintra, Portugal.
Dated Tuesday 2026-06-30 per photo caption. Hammack = Cleveland Fed president, 2026 FOMC VOTER.

## Key content
- "We've got inflation that's too high, and it's been too high for the past five years... if that
  continues, it may mean that we need higher interest rates to bring inflation back down to target."
- **"Insatiable" AI-infrastructure demand as an inflation source.** Cites a manufacturer in her district
  "involved in electric switching for data centers": "What they say is that the demand is insatiable,
  that these companies — these hyperscalers — **will pay almost any price for those inputs, and they
  need things built yesterday.**"
- **Transmission observation:** "particularly around large companies, I'm not seeing a lot of restraint
  in the economy. I'm not hearing from these businesses that interest rates or credit spreads are a
  reason why they're holding back from investment and growth."
- "If inflation continues to persist at these elevated levels and I don't see any restraint from policy,
  we may need to raise rates to bring that policy restraint in."
- Couched: "there could be impacts in both directions."
- **The Warsh split (article's framing):** her view "runs against a key assertion from Fed Chairman
  Kevin Warsh, who believes that productivity gains... will decrease the cost of labor and ultimately
  prove to be disinflationary." Warsh in first presser: firm commitment to bringing inflation down.
- ⚠️ Article's SEP line: FOMC "earlier this month voted again to keep its key overnight interest rate
  steady but **penciled in a quarter percentage point increase this year**, consistent with market
  expectations." (Cf. Bloomberg 7/9: "nine policymakers penciled in at least one quarter-point hike" —
  9/19 is not a median. Discrepancy: CNBC implies the SEP MEDIAN shows a 2026 hike. Resolve against the
  actual June SEP before treating the median-hike claim as fact.)
