# Ericsson tumbles on memory-chip cost inflation — margin warning
Source: ZeroHedge (Tyler Durden), Tue Jul 14 2026 04:20am ET. Pasted by Jake ~4:25am PT.
Quotes BBG Consensus, Citi (Gardiner), BNP Paribas, Barclays (Coles), Deutsche Bank (Menon).

## Key data
- Ericsson (Stockholm) fell as much as 10% (−7% after call finished) — most in 18 months.
- Outgoing CEO Börje Ekholm warned soaring component costs will pressure core Networks margins
  this quarter, partly driven by AI-fueled memory-chip demand.
- Q2 adj EBITA −7% YoY to SEK6.88B (BBG Consensus SEK6.82B — slight beat). Print fine; GUIDANCE
  the problem.
- Networks adj gross margin GUIDE 48–50% (BBG Consensus 49.5%).
- Cost-cutting: ~5,000 jobs cut 2025, similar targeted 2026; soft carrier spending.

## Citi (Andrew Gardiner)
- "The big challenge... the building component cost pressure and, not so much the near-term
  impact, but more the pressure to come in 2027." Top concern = margin impact extending into 2027.

## BNP Paribas (call takeaways)
1. Network-rollout cost drag: early quarters of a rollout cycle are most margin-demanding; mix
   shift to large rollouts (presume India/Japan) → ~100bp weaker Networks GM in Q3-26, drag for
   a few more qtrs. Accretive long-term. (idiosyncratic, NOT memory)
2. **Memory-cost inflation + LIMITED PASS-THROUGH: input costs rose in Q2, financial impact
   INCREASES over coming quarters. Most contracts long-term, LACK automatic price-pass-through
   clauses → cannot fully offset automatically. Pass-through gradual, case-by-case negotiation.
   "Weaker pricing power than we had appreciated... might not be able to fully pass on cost
   inflation this time."**
3. IPR settlement: marginal Q3 impact; IPR ARR up to SEK13.5bn (was 13.0bn).

## Barclays (Simon Coles)
- "Another quarter of resilient margins" but headwinds mounting in H2.

## Disclosure note
- Ericsson did NOT directly blame memory in release or on the call. DB analyst Janardan Menon
  PRESSED management on rising RAM prices + Chinese telecom competitive advantage (cheaper chips).
- CEO Ekholm: "there may be... a little bit lower cost inflation in the Chinese ecosystem. And...
  we cannot rely on that ecosystem to export to a number of countries we're in. That forces us
  to look at the product design in a different way."
