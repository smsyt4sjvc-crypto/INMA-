# "We've Burned Through All Buffers": Oil Traders Warn Market Running On Fumes
Source: ZeroHedge (citing FT, UBS, IEA, Energy Aspects, Natixis, Kpler, Bloomberg, Axios).
Dateline: Sat Jul 18 2026, 3:05pm ET. Captured via Jake's Safari share-PDF (full text extracted; chart
images not captured). Live URL 403s to bots (Cloudflare). Verbatim-adjacent extraction below.

- Brent crude futures jumped a little more than **4% to nearly $88/bbl**, on track for its **biggest weekly
  gain since April.** Followed an **Axios** report: Trump admin notified Israel it was deploying additional
  aerial assets to the region → US could expand strikes on Iran as soon as this weekend.
- **FT** spoke with energy traders end of week: slowing tanker traffic through Hormuz could trigger a **more
  severe supply crunch than the FIRST round** of the US-Iran war because emergency reserves/stockpiles that
  cushioned the earlier disruption are **mostly depleted.** Quote: *"We've burned through all of the buffers
  we had. Everything. All of that's now gone."*
- **Cushing remains at 'tank bottoms'.** Bloomberg: fuel markets strengthened again — **ICE gasoil crack
  closed at the highest ON RECORD; Nymex heating oil crack strongest since March.** Brent put spreads around
  the low $70s traded in sizable numbers.
- **IEA (earlier today):** member countries have released **three-quarters of the planned 400M-barrel**
  emergency reserves announced in March (≈300M released).
- **Amrita Sen (Energy Aspects founder):** heading into the US-Iran war, the global oil market had **~400M bbl
  of EXCESS inventories, ex-strategic reserves. "Now we have close to nothing... market complacency around
  Hormuz flows is being severely tested."**
- **UBS "Hormuz tracker" (Henri Patricot), UBS Evidence Lab data:**
  - Escalation: Iran targeted power plants + desalination plants in Kuwait (prior strikes were US military
    targets); followed US strikes on bridges + an airport in Iran.
  - **Oil+gas tanker crossings fell to ONE; only one product tanker entering the Gulf (Fig 1).**
  - **July-to-date crossings avg 10, down from mid-to-high teens (late June/early July), well below ~50 (Feb).**
  - Oil on water in the Gulf ticking up ~5Mb recently (Fig 10) — barrels stranded.
  - Absence of outbound flows: July avg **5.4 Mboe/d** vs **3.7 June** vs **1.3 May** (Fig 4).
  - Capacity entering the Gulf fell to **0.7 Mboe/d**, avg **5.2 MTD** (Fig 5).
  - **Bab al-Mandeb Strait NOT disrupted so far; flows INCREASED to 9.7Mb/d yesterday, above the 6.7Mb/d
    July-to-date avg.**
  - Gulf crude loadings ex-Iran fell sharply to **1.0Mb/d yesterday from 6.0Mb/d Wed**; past-week avg 3.2 vs
    5.1 July vs 3.4 June. **Iranian loadings ROSE to 5.0Mb/d yesterday** (July avg 1.5, vs typical 1.7-1.8).
  - Ports OUTSIDE the strait (Yanbu SA, Fujairah UAE) eased to **3.6Mb/d** from a rebound, below 5.9 JTD /
    6.9 June. Yanbu **2.7Mb/d** vs 4.2 MTD / 4.8 June. Product loadings inside the Gulf near May-June levels.
  - "Normalization pathway of tanker flows appears disrupted... Any sustained reopening of Hormuz now
    postponed."
- **"Great energy rewiring" (Hormuz bypass) — related pieces cited:** US supports Iraq-Syria oil pipeline to
  erode Tehran's Hormuz leverage; Dubai's new East Coast port; UAE "Zero Hormuz Dependency" race; **Saudi
  East-West pipeline hit a RECORD 7 MMb/d, UAE Fujairah crude loadings reached CAPACITY** ("bypasses maxed
  out"). Russia pressing a rail link to Iran & Azerbaijan to ease strain on maritime routes.
- **Natixis (Joel Hancock):** "the market was pricing an optimistic flow trajectory that now is clearly not on
  the table, at least... not until we get another round of diplomacy."
- **Kpler:** Strait crossings hit a 3-week low — **8 confirmed crossings on 16 July, 7 of the 8 used the
  Iranian route** (growing concentration on the Iran-side lane).
