# "Spotting Market Bubbles: Why History Says It's Nearly Impossible" — Lance Roberts / RIA
Source: RealInvestmentAdvice via ZH, pasted by Jake ~5:15am PT Jul 14 2026.

## Framework
- Paradox: if bubbles were spottable in real time they couldn't form. Valuation ≠ catalyst
  (Druckenmiller shorted Nikkei 1988, it ripped into late-89; "valuation is not a catalyst").
  Greenspan "irrational exuberance" 1996 → Nasdaq TRIPLED before 2000 peak. Bubbles sustained by
  NARRATIVE not arithmetic; error = taking a REAL trend and extrapolating past mean reversion.
- Lamont's FOUR HORSEMEN: (1) high prices/valuation, (2) high volatility (lurching, not drifting),
  (3) high volume esp. previously-inactive retail/speculative, (4) spread of "this time is
  different" bubble beliefs.
- Roberts' FIFTH: DEFENSIVENESS — when cheerleaders stop selling merits and start ATTACKING
  skeptics, top is near (1999 net stocks, 2021 SPAC/BTC, 2025 precious metals).
- Current vs 1999: CAPE within striking distance of Dec-1999 ATH; top-10 concentration >
  TMT at Mar-2000 peak; multiple normalization alone = 30%+ drawdown w/o recession. BUT leaders
  (NVDA/MSFT/GOOGL/META) throw off huge FCF vs Pets.com (9mo cash, burning VC). 13 indicators:
  4 similar/worse, 6 better, 3 watch. "Stretched market, single dominant narrative, severe
  concentration, but profitability/policy/retail better than the last top." Concentration = the
  real worry: "a thematic AI bet dressed as an index fund."
- END SIGNALS (what actually triggers unwind — liquidity tightening, not narrative change):
  (1) FLOOD OF NEW ISSUANCE (SPACs '21, net IPOs '99) — Lamont's #1 current watch, "AI-era
  giants preparing to go public... track week to week"; (2) buy-the-dip → BUY-THE-RIP FOMO;
  (3) mainstream coverage STOPS debating valuation (feature stories on momentum traders' personal
  lives) — "we aren't there yet but closer"; (4) a CREDIT EVENT — bubbles pop from the FINANCING
  CHAIN not the asset. "Next pop likely triggered by stress in PRIVATE CREDIT, LEVERAGED LOANS,
  or AI INFRASTRUCTURE FINANCING rather than the equity market itself."
- Prescription: stay diversified; trim winners/add laggards; hold differently-behaving assets
  (bonds/value/cash); DEFINE IN ADVANCE what forces you to cut risk, write it down; rebalancing
  ≠ timing. Bonds re-correlate negative in a true pop (growth+inflation both collapse).
- The debasement shot: Roberts published a critique of the "commodity supercycle and
  dollar-debasement thesis"; got dismissal + "wrong side of history" (not data) in reply; cites
  it as fifth-horseman defensiveness. Silver +135% on the year then biggest 1-day drop since
  1980s late-Jan 2026; gold −10% from peak same window.
