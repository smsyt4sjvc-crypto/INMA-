The Process

How It Works

Simple from your end. INMA handles the homeowner relationship — you handle the build.

1

INMA scopes and prices the job

We walk the property with the homeowner, build a detailed scope, and establish a fair market price. The homeowner retains INMA and commits to moving forward.

2

You receive the full packet

Scope of work, material takeoff, measurements, property photos, and a pre-written installation agreement with your company name and UBI already on it.

3

Three-way walkthrough

You, the homeowner, and Jake walk the property together. You confirm scope details and ask questions on-site before anyone signs anything.

4

Homeowner signs directly with you

The installation agreement is between you and the homeowner. INMA is not a party to it. The homeowner's deposit goes directly to you.

5

You run the job

You manage your crew, your schedule, your materials. INMA stays available for communication support but does not direct your work or means and methods.

What We Ask

The Standard

INMA personally vouches for every contractor in the network. That means we need to know you operate at a level we're comfortable putting our name next to.

  • Licensed, bonded, and insured — current WA contractor's license, general liability COI, and bond on file with INMA before any job is assigned.
  • Operationally sound — you're not taking jobs to make payroll from the last one. INMA won't send a homeowner's deposit to a cash-flow crisis.
  • Follow INMA's installation standards — James Hardie Best Practices, proper flashing, manufacturer specs. The warranty stays intact. No shortcuts.
  • Use INMA's contract with the homeowner — consistent paperwork protects everyone. The agreement is pre-written with your details. You review it, you sign it.
  • Priority scheduling for INMA jobs — when a job is ready, it's ready. Contractors who consistently pass or delay lose access to future jobs.
