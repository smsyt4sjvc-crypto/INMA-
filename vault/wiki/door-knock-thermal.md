# door-knock-thermal — free thermal scan as a door-opener

> An idea Jake is actively weighing (2026-07-20): knock doors offering a free
> thermal scan + report in exchange for a signed release, then either sell the
> data or use it to build a warm list.

Related: [[marketing]] · [[positioning]] · [[_guardrails]] · [[decisions]] · [[state]]
Source: conversation 2026-07-20

## DATA (what's true / the capability)
- [2026-07-20] The **Home Tune-Up (~$750)** already includes a full thermal scan
  to find energy loss — so Jake already owns the tool/skill; the free scan is a
  slice of an existing paid service used as a lead magnet.
- [2026-07-20] The site already references **Avista rebates** (windows card) — a
  utility relationship/angle already exists.
- [2026-07-20] The **cold 250-piece mailer produced ~0 trackable response**
  ([[marketing]]) — cold lists are the weak point this could replace.

## IDEAS & DIRECTION (thinking — not yet fact)
- [2026-07-20] **Strongest use = lead-gen + first-party warm list.** A thermal
  image of heat escaping a wall is undeniable visual proof — creates the need and
  proves expertise with no selling. Sorts the market (bad scan = needs
  siding/windows/insulation). Builds a list of homes Jake has physically been to
  with a known problem — far better than any purchased list. On-brand ("I find the
  problem before the salesman does, and I'm on your side"). Feeds Tune-Ups +
  full scope.
- [2026-07-20] **Weakest leg = "sell data to the city."** Uncertain buyer, long
  procurement cycle, privacy review; and it can undercut the advocate/trust brand
  ("scanned free, sold your data"). Reframe: the realistic data buyer/partner is
  the **utility (Avista)** via rebate/weatherization programs, not the city — and
  even that is a maybe, not the reason to do this. Keep the data angle OUT of the
  door pitch; the pitch is "free scan, free report, no obligation."
- [2026-07-20] **Enabling artifacts to build:** (1) a GC-safe consent/release form
  (informational energy scan, NOT a home inspection, no warranty; clear on
  image/data use + any resale); (2) a branded Thermal Scan Report template
  (matches Home Tune-Up look); (3) simple lead-capture that feeds future
  texts/mailers.
- [2026-07-20] **Check first:** Spokane may require a solicitor's permit for
  door-to-door.
