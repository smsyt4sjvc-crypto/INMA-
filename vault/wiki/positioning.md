# positioning — how INMA works and makes money

> The core idea and the operating/money model. The thing that must stay
> consistent everywhere. One concept: what INMA *is*.

## DATA (what's true now)

### The core idea
- [2026-07-10] INMA is a **homeowner advocate / buyer's agent for exterior
  remodeling projects**. Jake represents the homeowner, prices the job to fair
  market, and presents *their offer* to a network of vetted local contractors.
  He is **not a contractor**.
- [2026-07-10] The analogy that lands: like an **insurance agent** — you hire
  them, they shop the market, and they're paid a commission out of the deal. Same
  here, except the fee comes out of the project budget, not tacked on top. The
  homeowner's total cost stays the same; they get someone in their corner.
- [2026-07-10] The value flip ("leverage / hindsight ahead of time"): a homeowner
  remodels a few times in a life; contractors sell every day. INMA puts a
  repeat-player on the homeowner's side *before* the mistake.

### The money model
- [2026-07-10] INMA's fee: **10–15% of the total offer**, by scope complexity.
- [2026-07-10] The **homeowner pays INMA directly and upfront** — retained at
  signing. INMA is paid by the homeowner, **not** the contractor.
- [2026-07-10] The fee is a visible **contract debit** carved out of the offer
  *before* the contractor's number is set: homeowner's total = the offer amount;
  contractor accepts a number already net of the fee; shown in the open. Net
  effect: the contractor effectively bears it (reduced proceeds), homeowner is
  payer of record, homeowner's total cost does not increase.
- [2026-07-10] INMA **never collects, holds, or disburses construction funds.**
  Construction payments go homeowner → contractor directly, under a separate
  installation agreement.

### How it operates
- [2026-07-10] INMA is an independent **scoping-and-sales firm**: Jake scopes the
  full job (materials + labor), prices to fair market, and packages "the offer" —
  a complete, ready-to-sign contract. A vetted contractor **accepts or declines as
  is** (no renegotiating homeowner terms). Jake is the homeowner's single point of
  contact.

### Ways to work with INMA
- [2026-07-10] (1) DIY Assistance — coaching (from $250 visit · $250/wk · $75/house
  call). (2) Standalone Proposal — scope+price, homeowner shops it, flat fee.
  (3) Full Scope — the complete buyer's-agent service. (4) Home Tune-Up —
  *Inspect · Seal · Clean · Report*, year-round, ~$750.

## IDEAS & DIRECTION (thinking — not yet fact)
- [2026-07-10] Contractor gate discussed as **credential-based and free** (license,
  bond, insurance + references/photos), not pay-to-play — chosen to keep money
  one-directional and avoid liability. A "$300 upfront OR deduct full 12%"
  homeowner-fee structure was floated; confirm the settled numbers before baking
  them into copy.
