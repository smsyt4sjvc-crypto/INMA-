## DATA (the rules — verbatim from repo-root CLAUDE.md)

### Say this
- "I'm not a contractor. I'm your personal homeowner advocate."
- "Like a buyer's agent for your project. I work for you, not the crew doing the work."
- "You make the offer. INMA presents it as your offer to our network of vetted
  local crews. They accept your terms or they pass."
- "My fee comes out of the contract price — deducted dollar-for-dollar, in front
  of you. Not added on top."
- "Fair-market pricing you can verify yourself."
- "Hire the right guy, the first time."

### Never say this (liability + brand)
- ❌ "We handle the match, scope, and accountability" / "we control the work" /
  anything implying INMA is responsible for construction outcomes → creates
  **general-contractor liability**. INMA coordinates and advocates; the licensed
  contractor is responsible for the work.
- ❌ "I shred my estimate" / "uniform contract" — sounds shady, implies GC control.
- ❌ "We'll beat any quote by 20% guaranteed" → soften to "I can very often reduce
  these by around 20%."
- ❌ Seasonal-only language for Home Tune-Ups — it's **year-round**.
- ❌ Presenting the estimate as a binding contractor bid — it's an **estimate of
  fair-market cost** and the homeowner's **offer**.

### The liability line (holds everywhere)
INMA is **not** a contractor, GC, or construction manager. It does not control,
supervise, or guarantee the work, and **never collects, holds, or disburses
construction funds.** Money is one-directional: the homeowner pays INMA's fee
upfront; construction payments go homeowner → contractor directly.

### Standard disclosure (verbatim where a disclaimer is needed)
> "INMA is not a contractor. INMA presents this as your offer to our network of
> vetted local crews as an advocate for our clients. This is an estimate of
> fair-market cost for the scope described above."

## DATA (voice)
- Punchy, plainspoken, blue-collar-premium. Pacific Northwest craftsman.
- Confident, not salesy or hype-y. Honest about tradeoffs. Trusted local expert,
  not tech startup. No corporate-speak, no neon, no exclamation-point hype.
